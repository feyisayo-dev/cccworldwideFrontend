History of Celestial Church of Christ

Welcome to CCC Worldwide

The Celestial Church of Christ World-wide was divinely established on September 29, 1947, in Porto Novo, Republic of Benin (formerly Republic of Dahomey) by the Reverend Pastor Prophet Founder, Samuel Bilehou Joseph Oshoffa. The church's inception is considered a divine occurrence, marked by a heavenly order and divine revelation.

The Divine Order

On that fateful day in 1947, during deep prayer, a winged angel, radiant with intense light, appeared to the Founder. It was then that God’s message came: “It is the wish of God to send you on an errand of preaching to the world. Many nominal Christians, when faced with the problems of this world, turn to fetish priests and powers of darkness for help. As a result, on their death, they cannot see Christ because Satan has left his spiritual mark on them. To assist you in your work, miraculous works of holy divine healing will be carried out in the name of Jesus Christ. These miracles, along with God’s mark on you, will testify that you are sent by God.” Thus, the Celestial Church of Christ was born.

Founding and Revelation of the Church's Name

The name of the Church, “Eglise du Christianisme Céleste” (Celestial Church of Christ), was revealed through Mr. Alexander Yanga. He was undergoing spiritual healing and was in a trance for seven days. On the seventh day, he requested a piece of chalk and inscribed the name on the wall.

Headquarters

CCC Holy Land Imeko, Nigeria

The Church has its Supreme Headquarters in Porto Novo, Republic of Benin, its National (Diocesan) Headquarters at 12/15 Church Street, Makoko, Yaba, Lagos State, Nigeria, and its International Headquarters at Mission House, Ketu, Lagos State, Nigeria.

The Founder’s Role

Samuel Oshoffa
Founding pastor

Apart from being a prophet, the Founder was given permission by divine order to use the angelic title "EPASTORAL," which was translated to “Pastor.” This occurred after he spent five years as an “Evangelist” following the establishment of the Church.
